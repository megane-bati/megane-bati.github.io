About - more info on LinkedIn

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Storm team at IRIT, under the supervision of Nicolas Mellado and Mathias Paulin. I am working on the ANR MCG-Rad project, which aims at evaluating the radiative forcing of an ensemble of greenhouse gases, at the global scale and integrated over climatic durations, using Monte-Carlo methods. Especially, I am working on the data structure to access the gas absorption spectrum which is critical for the computation time. This work is conducted in close collaboration with the GREPHE team at LaPlaCE.

I defended my Ph.D. thesis in December 2021, advised by Romain Pacanowski and Pascal Barla at LP2N, about the inverse design of layered material appearance in the context of geometric optics. We propose a method allowing, from appearance properties such as color and sharpness of reflections, to compute a set of physical parameter configurations producing this expected appearance. This work was part of the ANR VIDA project, and partly conducted at Inria Bordeaux Sud-Ouest in the Manao team.

I am interested in physically-based rendering, in particular material appearance modeling and Monte-Carlo methods.
